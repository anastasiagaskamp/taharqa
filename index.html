In front of the sarcophagus is placed a basket with several possessions, all of which are needed for the Egyptian afterlife. More information can be found below.

Hieroglyphs

Translation: Here lies Taharqa

Booby Trap

This trap works through being stones patterned across the floor. It becomes activated through a person stepping on the wrong symbol. If the wrong symbol is stepped on, the stone moves away to show a hole in the floor, causing the person to fall into a pit, which contains spikes at the bottom that should kill the person once they hit the bottom. If the person gets across successfully, they enter the tomb.

Possessions

Items:

  • Shabti
    • This item was chosen because of Egyptian beliefs about the afterlife. According to their beliefs, they would continue to work in the afterlife. The shabti were created to help them with tasks, as, in the afterlife, they would come to life to help their owner.
  • Scarab
    • This item was also chosen because of Egyptian beliefs about the afterlife. According to their beliefs, they had to be 'pure' to make it into the afterlife. The scarab protects the person's heart from when they lie, and say that they didn't do something that they actually did do.
  • Bread
    • This item was chosen for the Egyptian afterlife because, in their afterlife, the person would need food. With bread being a basic foodstuff for the Egyptians, this was a good choice for the afterlife.

Paintings

from left to right

  • This shows Taharqa ruling over his people, who are represented to be much smaller than him because of their lack of importance.
  • This shows Anubis, who is known as a god of the dead. He did the weighing of the soul in Egyptian mythology, in which the person's soul is weighed with the feather.
  • This final painting shows Osiris, the god of the dead.

Sarcophagus

This sarcophagus is placed near the back of this tomb, on top of a stone slab. In front of this sarcophagus is a basket with several possessions needed for the afterlife.
